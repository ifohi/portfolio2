What is Store.it?

It’s a SaaS web application that’s a go-to place to
easily store, organize and share information. It has a
clean & functional interface where users can start
using it at the first login.

Problem

Need a convenient place to save and share online
content, such as notes, links and images

Solution

Design a web application where users can easily
save and share information. The app must be easy to
use and accessible anywhere.

User Research

I started my research by conducting an online survey in order to understand the
users and to find out what they’re looking for in a cloud storage system.
Majority of users are already utilizing cloud storage to some degree. One pain
point is that they often “dump” their content into the dashboard so they’re
looking for an efficient way to organize their files. With these factors in mind, I
went onto competitive analysis of the current cloud storage market.

86%

of users utilize cloud storage

72%

of those users utilize Google Drive

84%

of users want something that’s free

92%

68%

Competitive Analysis

I SWOT analyzed several of the top cloud service companies: Google Drive, Dropbox, and
Amazon Drive. Google Drive offers a generous amount of free storage before upgrading to paid plan;
however their dashboard has a busy feel with many features. On the other hand, Dropbox has a
polished interface with an intuitive dashboard but their paid plans are more expensive than
other options. Amazon Drive has unlimited photo storage, but users must first be paid members.
With these features in mind, I focused on a simple yet functional product for users to organize their contents.

Bottom Line: What do users wants?

  1. Service that is free and affordable
  2. App that’s accessible on whatever device, wherever they are
  3. Efficient way to organize the collected information

User Persona

After the research phase, I created user personas to represent the goals and
behaviors of potential users.

I'm stuck at my office, because some files are online and some aren't. I want to migrate all my files
to be online but my free cloud storage is reaching its limit additional storage is pricey.

GOALS

I want to work wherever, not just in the office. I'd like to sign up for affordable additional storage.

“I want to keep up with technology to keep up with my growing business.”

Sarah

24 y/o marketing assistant from California

FRUSTRATIONS

I keep track of the team's files, but the number of files grow daily. I need something to help me organize,
but I don't have time to learn a new file management system.

GOALS

I want a system with an easy learning curve and can start using on the first login.

“I don’t have time to learn a new cloud storage system - I want something that’s easy to learn.”

Branding

In order to develop the concept, I did a mind map on “organize” and “storage.”
This helped me to generate various logo sketches, which I then converted into vector drawings
using Sketch. Along with submitting these vectors for feedback from fellow mentors and colleagues,
I did A/B testing to determine the final design. The final design was inspired by a library catalog as it
holds a large volume of information within a compact space, which is what Store.it offers to
users.

User Testing Iterations

After wireframing, I created high-fidelity mockups. During this step of the design process,
“iterate” was my mantra. For my homepage’s v.1, I got feedback from mentors and colleauges that my spacing, grids,
color, and layout needed to improve. So, after several versions, my final design included
angles to break up the monotony of the layout, to incorporate more of my color palette, and
to strategically use white space.

The dashboard also required several user testing and iterations. I wanted the dashboard to have a clean, bright vibe, but I overdid it as I got responses that it’s too white and harsh on the eyes and the proportions seemed off (v.1). So, I modified the dashboard’s dimensions andI added a darker top and left-side nav bar (v.2), and eventually used red to distinguish the two header sections (v.3). I was satisfied with v.3 , but after another round of testing, users’ comments compelled me to make more changes.

Users said it was jarring to see clear images next to plain text. It was also hard to see the little icon in the top left-corner to indicate the file type. So based on the feedback, in v.4, I added gray tiles at lower opacity with the file icon in the middle to help users see right away what kind of file it is and to give the dashboard a more uniform look. I also lightened the dashboard as majority of users wanted something “less constricting” and “airer.”

What I Learned

Going back to the drawing board countless times was initially a major bummer. In retrospect, though, going back to further brainstorm and sketch pushed me to generate better ideas, which in the end is a huge plus for users. Also, I learned to keep my user personas handy as I frequently referred to it. Even after iterations, I double-checked with the personas to ensure I’m staying focused on the users’ wants. Along with remembering my personas, I definitely needed to test, test, test and let the data speak for itself instead of letting my bias slip in. If given more time, I’d like to include a welcome tour at the first onboarding experience where users have the option to get a quick tour of the features.
